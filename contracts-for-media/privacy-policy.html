Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze user behavior, and improve the functionality of our applications and services.

Below, we explain what these technologies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your preferences.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you use our apps or visit our website.

These files allow us to recognize your device and remember your preferences over time.

In addition to cookies, we may use other technologies, such as:

  • Web Beacons: Small image files embedded in web pages or emails to monitor user interactions.
  • Device Identifiers: Unique identifiers, such as IDFA or Android Advertising ID, used to track usage patterns.
  • Analytics Tools: Technologies like Google Analytics and Firebase Analytics to gather usage data and trends.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies and tracking technologies:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic functionality of our applications and services.
  • Performance Cookies: Help us monitor and improve the performance of our apps, such as load times and error reports.
  • Analytics Cookies: Collect data about user interactions to analyze usage patterns and improve app functionality.
  • Advertising Cookies: Enable us to provide targeted ads and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.

3. How We Use Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and tracking technologies for the following purposes:

  • To remember your preferences and settings.
  • To analyze app performance and optimize user experience.
  • To deliver personalized content and recommendations.
  • To monitor the effectiveness of marketing campaigns.
  • To track errors, crashes, and app performance through tools like Firebase Crashlytics and Google Analytics.

4. Managing Cookies and Tracking Preferences

You have control over how cookies and tracking technologies are used. Here are your options:

  • Browser Settings: You can adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies.
  • In-App Settings: Some of our applications provide in-app controls to manage cookies and tracking preferences.

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality and usability of our apps and services.

5. Third-Party Cookies

We also use third-party cookies provided by services like Google Analytics and Facebook SDK.

These cookies are used to:

  • Track user interactions across platforms.
  • Provide aggregated data for performance analysis.
  • Deliver targeted advertisements based on user interests.

For more information on how these third parties handle your data, please refer to their respective privacy policies:

Security Measures

At 618Media, we are committed to ensuring the security of your personal information.

We implement a variety of technical, administrative, and physical safeguards to protect your data from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure.

We use state-of-the-art technology and encryption techniques to secure your data during transmission and storage.

These include:

  • Data Encryption: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using Secure Socket Layer (SSL) or Transport Layer Security (TLS) protocols.

  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct periodic security assessments and vulnerability scans to identify and mitigate risks.
  • Access Controls: Role-based access ensures that only authorized personnel can access sensitive data.

2. Administrative Safeguards

Our internal policies and procedures are designed to promote data security and ensure compliance with applicable laws and standards.

These include:

  • Employee Training: All team members are trained on data protection policies and best practices to ensure the responsible handling of personal data.
  • Incident Response Plan: A comprehensive plan is in place to address security breaches or incidents promptly and effectively.

3. Physical Safeguards

In addition to digital protections, we implement physical security measures to safeguard the infrastructure where your data is stored. These include:

  • Secure data centers with access control systems and surveillance.
  • Backup systems stored in secure, offsite locations.
  • Environmental controls to protect against physical damage from fire, flooding, or power outages.

4. Protection Against Unauthorized Access

We take proactive steps to prevent unauthorized access to your data, including:

  • Using multi-factor authentication (MFA) for administrative accounts.
  • Monitoring system activity and maintaining logs for suspicious behavior.
  • Regularly updating software and applying security patches to address vulnerabilities.

5. Limitations of Security Measures

While we strive to protect your data, it is important to note that no system can be 100% secure.

We encourage you to take precautions, such as:

  • Using strong, unique passwords for your accounts.
  • Keeping your devices and software up to date with the latest security updates.

6. Reporting Security Concerns

7. Ongoing Commitment

We are dedicated to maintaining the highest standards of security and regularly review and update our security practices to adapt to emerging threats and advancements in technology.
